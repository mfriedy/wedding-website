If you decide to extend your holiday, there are inter-island flights from Honolulu airport. Southwest and Hawaiian airlines fly regularly to each island, with most inter-island flights being less than an hour. If you decide to visit Kauai or the Big Island, ask us for recommendations!

Transport

Uber is used on Oahu and is easily available. It is about a 20-30 minute Uber from Honolulu airport to Waikiki Beach.

Rental cars are also available for hire on Oahu if you would like to explore other areas of the island. Please note, parking in Waikiki Beach could prove expensive and difficult.

Transport will not be provided to or from the wedding venue.

Hotels

The wedding ceremony and reception will be held at Halekulani Hotel in Waikiki Beach. We will be staying here only on the 9th of October, but will be staying nearby at the Embassy Suites by Hilton on Waikiki Beach (currently approx US$330 p/night) the other nights. The wedding venue offers a 20% discount on Halekulani Hotel (~$800 to $1,200 USD per night) and the sister hotel, Halepuna Hotel (~$405 to $600 USD per night). Please contact us if you would like to use the discount

The Waikiki Beach area has almost 100 hotels, varing from luxury hotels right on the beach to more affordable hotels a few blocks back. There are both chain hotels and small independent hotels, with a range of facilities (e.g. including kitchens). Please book what best fits your needs and ask us if you need any assistance.

You may wish to also look further afield in wider Honolulu for accommodation, if you want an even wider range of options. Whilst being further away from the wedding activities, this could offer some additional alternatives.

Airbnbs are heavily regulated on Oahu. They are limited to very specific areas on the island. If you decide to stay in an airbnb, please ensure the booking information includes the government rental code, meaning it is approved for short term rentals.

Things to do on Oahu

Please let us know if you plan to visit Kauai or the Big Island (Hawaii) and we can happily pass on our recommendations! If you're going to Maui, Lanai or Molokai we can't help you!

Diamond Head

Diamond Head is a volcanic tuff cone on Oahu. The Summit Trail takes about two hours.

Pearl Harbor Memorial and Museum

Visit the Pearl Harbor Memorial and Museum where the attack happened in 1941. You can learn more about the unexpected attack on the island, as well as take a ferry to view the USS Arizona. The wreck is still underwater of the old 'Battleship Row'. It is close to Honolulu Airport.

Snorkeling

Hawaii has beautiful snorkeling opportunities, with plenty of coral and marine life to see. There are plenty of snorkel tours that leave from Waikiki or Prince Wharf.
